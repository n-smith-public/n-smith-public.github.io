How did the OA form?

In 1915, E. Urner Goodman, a field executive for the Philadelphia Council, was assigned to serve as director of the council's summer camp at Treasure Island Scout Reservation. He believed that the summer camp experience should do more than just teach proficiency in Scoutcraft skills; rather, the principles embodied in the Scout Oath and Scout Law should become realities in the lives of Scouts. Along with his assistant camp director, Carroll A. Edson, he started an experimental honor society to acknowledge those campers he felt best exemplified these qualities. In 1934 Goodman's program became nationally recognized by BSA.Toward the end of the twentieth century, the OA expanded its focus to include conservation, high adventure, and servant-leadership.

How can I join the OA?

To join the Order of the Arrow you must meet the requirements below. They are not hard guidelines, but what makes joining hard is the ordeal. The ordeal is an event which prepares you to join the Order of the Arrow. Lodge elections can occur whenever your unit leaders meet up with the Lodge election officials to schedule an election, however there is a maximum of one election per calendar year.

  • Be a registered member of the Boy Scouts of America (Scouts BSA, Venturing, or Sea Scouts)
  • Have at least 15 camping nights within two years of being elected. These must include one long term campout (Over 5 days) - Typically Summer Camp, and the rest can not be longer than 3 days.
  • Must be under the age of 21 before election within your unit.
  • Meet the minimum rank requirements. For Scouts BSA, this is First Class. For Venturing, this is Discovery rank. For Sea Scouts, this is Ordinary rank.
  • Be elected by a majority of your fellow youth members.

If you are over 21 and wish to join the OA, you must still meet the first two requirements, but instead must be nominated to and approved by your local Lodge Adult Selection Committee.
